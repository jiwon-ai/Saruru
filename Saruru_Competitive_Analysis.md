# Saruru — Competitive & Market Analysis (money lens)

> Goal of this doc: look at similar apps and the top earners, and pull out **what actually makes money** — so Saruru is built to earn, not just to exist. All figures are third-party estimates (Sensor Tower, Business of Apps, RevenueCat, Adapty, Statista) and rounded; treat as directional, verify before betting on them. Dated June 2026.

---

## 1. Bottom line

Three things the market is telling us:

1. **The giants are shrinking; focused niche apps are thriving.** Total wellness-app revenue *fell* ~6% in 2025 to ~$848M, and Calm/Headspace downloads dropped 60–70% from their peak. Meanwhile small, focused apps (Finch, Daylio) and AI journals (Rosebud, Mindsera) are growing. **Lesson: don't fight Calm. Be a sharp niche.**
2. **AI reflection commands a premium price.** Generic mood trackers charge ~$4.99/mo; AI journaling apps charge **$12.99–14.99/mo** for essentially the same loop plus AI. Saruru's AI reframe sits in the *premium* bracket — the €4.99 assumption is probably **underpriced.**
3. **Retention, not signups, is the whole game.** Health-app Day-30 retention is ~3–10%. Saruru's episodic-use risk (Risk A) is exactly this problem. The money goes to whoever turns a one-off into a habit — gently.

## 4. What actually makes money (5 playbook lessons)

1. **Generous free tier + soft paywall beats a hard wall.** Finch lets you fully raise the pet for free and sells customization/extras. Gating core utility (a Daylio criticism) drives churn. → Saruru: the *burn ritual itself* should be free and satisfying; sell the AI depth.
2. **AI is a pricing lever, not just a cost.** AI journals charge $12.99–14.99. Saruru's reframe is the premium feature — price the AI tier above mood-tracker rates (think **$7.99–9.99/mo**, not €4.99), and protect margin with the non-AI free tier + credits.
3. **Annual plans win.** Users are 57% more likely to pick yearly; annual subs retain ~33%/yr vs. ~17% monthly. → Lead with the annual price, anchor monthly as the expensive option.
4. **Longer free trials convert better.** 17–32-day trials convert ~45.7% vs. ~26.8% for ≤4-day trials. → Give a real trial window, not 3 days.
5. **A gentle habit mechanic lifts retention.** Finch's pet turns a wellness chore into a daily return. Saruru needs a *non-addictive* equivalent (a calm streak / "residue released" counter) — without gamifying away the dignity of the moment.

---

## 5. Pricing & packaging recommendation for Saruru

- **Free (no AI):** unlimited basic text burn + the ritual. Near-zero cost, builds habit, the share-able hook.
- **Plus (AI):** **~€7.99/mo or ~€49.99/yr** (raise from €4.99 — the AI justifies it; benchmark to Rosebud/Mindsera, not Daylio). Annual front-and-center.
- **Trial:** 14–30 days, not 3.
- **Credits:** only if/when cloud STT is added (per the v1 decision).
- **Benchmark to beat:** health-app median revenue-per-install ≈ **$0.63** after 60 days. Model your funnel against that; AI premium pricing can exceed it *if* retention holds.

---

## 6. The niche gap — and where the money really is

**The gap is real:** hospitality (52%) and retail (48%) have the highest rates of stressed/anxious workers; ~1 in 4 hospitality workers report a mental-health issue. Yet support is mostly **nonprofits** (CHOW, Behind You / Southern Smoke) and **employer wellness platforms** (Calm Business, Modern Health, Wysa for orgs) — **no dedicated consumer app for post-incident emotional decompression.**

**But read the money carefully:** in this exact segment, dollars have historically flowed through **B2B/employer budgets**, not direct worker subscriptions. That reinforces the plan's hardest risk — *the people with the most pain have the least disposable income* (Risk B) — and the hypothesis that **the paying buyer may be the employer.** Translation: the consumer app is the wedge and the brand; serious revenue may require an eventual employer-paid path (handled carefully, individual entries always private).

---

## 7. Hard truths reconfirmed by the data

- **Acquisition is expensive and incumbents are retreating.** Finch tests *hundreds* of ad creatives monthly. A solo founder can't outspend — **organic (founder story, TikTok) is the only viable wedge.** Matches the plan.
- **Retention is the make-or-break metric**, and Saruru's episodic use is the weakest spot. Validate frequency before betting on subscriptions (Risk A).
- **Thin moat.** Mood-tracker + AI prompt + animation is copyable. The defensibility is niche brand + reframe quality (Risk F).
- **B2B is a bigger pool but a slower, different business** with its own privacy contradiction (Risk D).

---

## 8. Recommended money strategy (synthesis)

1. **Position as a focused niche tool, Finch/Daylio-style — not a Calm competitor.**
2. **Free ritual that builds a gentle habit; sell AI depth at a premium ($7.99–9.99/mo), annual-first, with a 14–30 day trial.**
3. **Protect margin:** non-AI free tier, AI only for payers, credits later for STT.
4. **Win on organic distribution** (the torn-coupon founder story is your cheapest, strongest asset).
5. **Treat employer-paid B2B as the eventual revenue engine**, but only after the consumer wedge proves retention — and never at the cost of individual privacy.
6. **Obsess over first-week retention and incident frequency** before scaling spend; that single metric decides whether this is a business or a feature.
